History

The popular legend about the discovery of coffee tells that a goatherd named Kaldi, who lived in Ethiopia in the 9th century, noticed that his goats became more energetic after eating the fruits of a certain plant. Curious, Kaldi tried the and felt an increase in his own energy. He shared his discovery with a local monk, who used the fruit to make a drink that helped him stay awake during long hours of prayer. This drink spread the region and was called "qahwa," which means "energizer" in Arabic.

Coffee's popularity continued to grow in the Arabian region and eventually reached Egypt, where it became an important part of the culture. In the 15th century, cafés (or qahveh khaneh) began to appear in cities such as Istanbul, Constantinople and other parts of the Middle East. other parts of the Middle East. These places served coffee and became social centers where people gathered to discuss various topics.

Coffee spread throughout Europe in the 17th century, when the first coffee houses were opened in Venice and then in other European cities. During the 18th century, coffee plantations were established in various European colonies, such as the Caribbean islands and Brazil. Caribbean islands and Brazil, further boosting coffee production and consumption around the world.

It's important to note that coffee has a complex history and several versions of its origin, but the legend of Kaldi is one of the best known.

As for the "founder" of coffee, there is no single person who can be credited as its founder. Coffee is a discovery that evolved over time, passing through various cultures and societies before becoming the popular drink we know today.

Why drink coffee?

Stimulant: Coffee contains caffeine, a natural stimulant that can increase alertness, improve focus and combat fatigue. Many people consume coffee to help them stay awake and alert, especially during work, studies or long journeys.

Taste and aroma: Coffee is appreciated for its unique taste and aroma. Different varieties of coffee have distinct flavor profiles, with notes that can include fruit, chocolate, spices and even floral notes. People often drink coffee for the pleasure.

Social ritual: Coffee plays an important role in many cultures as a social drink. People often gather in coffee shops or at home to share a cup of coffee and chat. This socializing ritual is a significant part of the coffee experience.

Energy and productivity: Many people believe that coffee helps them to be more productive and combat drowsiness. It can be consumed to improve performance in mental and physical tasks.

Comforting sensation: For many people, a hot cup of coffee can offer a feeling of comfort and coziness, especially on cold days.

Creativity and inspiration: Some people report that coffee helps unleash creativity and inspiration. Many writers, artists and creative professionals drink coffee while working.

It's important to note that coffee consumption should be done in moderation, as too much caffeine can lead to negative side effects such as insomnia, nervousness and heart palpitations. In addition, caffeine tolerance can vary from person to person. person. It is therefore advisable to know your own limits and consume coffee responsibly.
